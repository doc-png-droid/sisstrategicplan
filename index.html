About Action Plans & WASC Accreditation

This Strategic Action Plan is a living document developed in direct response to recommendations from SIS's WASC (Western Association of Schools and Colleges) mid-cycle review. WASC accreditation requires schools to maintain a continuous cycle of self-study, goal-setting, and evidence-based improvement. Action plans under WASC serve as structured commitments — each identifying responsible parties, timelines, required resources, and measurable success indicators. They are reviewed regularly by school leadership and shared with the wider community to ensure transparency, accountability, and sustained progress toward the school's mission. Every division (Elementary, Middle, and High School) contributes to each priority area, ensuring that improvement is coherent and school-wide.

🏫

About the Two Areas of Focus

This plan is structured across two interconnected dimensions. The first addresses student-facing programs — well-being, curriculum, data systems, and community engagement — ensuring every learner is supported, challenged, and seen. The second focuses on staff and professional growth — building teacher leadership, deepening instructional practice, and sustaining a culture of collective efficacy. Both dimensions are essential and mutually reinforcing.

Enhanced Student Support and Well-being

Mental health, SEL, restorative practices, and intervention systems across all divisions

▼
